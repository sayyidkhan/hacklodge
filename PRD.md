# HackLodge

## One-Liner

Builder infrastructure for hackathons.

---

# Vision

Enable hackathon participants to sleep, recharge, and build without leaving the venue.

---

# Problem

Hackathons often run for 24–72 hours.

Participants face:

* No designated sleeping areas
* Fatigue and burnout
* Dead devices
* Lack of secure storage
* Poor recovery facilities
* Difficulty maintaining productivity throughout the event

Meanwhile, organizers struggle to provide these services without adding operational complexity.

---

# Solution

HackLodge transforms existing venue spaces into temporary builder infrastructure.

Instead of sending participants to hotels or hostels, HackLodge creates:

* Sleep Zones
* Charging Zones
* Locker Zones
* Recovery Zones
* Gear Rental Stations

directly within or adjacent to the hackathon venue.

# Core Services

## Sleep Zone

Dedicated quiet areas for rest.

Includes:

* Sleeping mats
* Foldable beds
* Blankets
* Eye masks
* Ear plugs

---

## Charging Zone

Centralized charging stations.

Includes:

* Power strips
* Fast chargers
* USB-C charging
* Device monitoring

---

## Locker Zone

Secure storage during the event.

Includes:

* Backpack storage
* Equipment storage
* QR-based check-in/out

---

## Recovery Zone

Designed to reduce fatigue.

Includes:

* Beanbags
* Water stations
* Snacks
* Stretching area

---

## Builder Gear Rental

Participants can rent:

* Monitors
* Mechanical keyboards
* Mice
* Laptop stands
* Power banks
* Extension cords
